# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist for College Students (India)

## General Online Safety

1. **Always verify the URL before entering any login credentials.** Look for `https://` and the exact domain name. A single letter difference (e.g., `sbi-bank.in` instead of `sbi.co.in`) is a red flag.

2. **Never click on links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown senders.** Go directly to the official website by typing the URL in your browser.

3. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA prevents access.

4. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password has at least 12 characters with a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed.

5. **Do not share OTPs with anyone — ever.** No bank, government portal, or company will ask for your OTP over a phone call or message.

6. **Be cautious with public Wi-Fi.** Avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi networks in cafes, libraries, or railway stations. Use mobile data for financial transactions.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety (Especially for Indian Students)

7. **UPI receive requests do NOT require your PIN.** If someone asks you to enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money, it is a scam — entering your PIN will debit money from your account, not credit it.

8. **Never scan a QR code sent by a stranger** claiming to send you money. Scanning a payment QR code means you are paying them, not receiving from them.

---

## Social & Identity Safety

9. **Audit your social media privacy settings every 3 months.** Limit who can see your personal information, friend list, location, and posts.

10. **Do not post photos of your ID cards, admit cards, or bank passbooks** on social media — even partially visible ones. This information can be used for identity theft.

---

## Report Cybercrime

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (24x7, available across India)
- Report incidents immediately — early reporting increases the chances of recovering funds or stopping further damage.

---

> *Remember: No legitimate authority, bank, or service will pressure you to act immediately online. Urgency is a manipulation tactic. Pause, verify, then act.*
