## Reflection (150–200 words)

For this task, I worked with two sets of platforms — HackerRank for coding practice, and Google Forms for collaboration.

On HackerRank, I completed the "Solve Me First" challenge. Even though it was a simple addition problem, the experience of reading a problem statement, writing a solution, and submitting it through an online judge taught me the workflow that competitive programmers use daily. I plan to continue solving problems on HackerRank as I progress through my programming courses.

For Part B, I created a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms. I designed questions that range from factual multiple-choice (about phishing and safe passwords) to open-ended short answers (about suspicious links). Linking the form to a Google Sheet allowed me to see how responses are collected automatically — a feature that is genuinely useful for surveys, feedback collection, and quizzes in academic settings.

Both tools have direct academic utility. HackerRank will help me prepare for technical interviews and coding rounds in the future, while Google Forms and Sheets are immediately useful for organising group projects, collecting peer feedback, and conducting surveys as part of research assignments.
