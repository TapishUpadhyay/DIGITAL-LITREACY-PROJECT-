## Infographic Description (for Report Reference)

**Tool Used:** Canva (canva.com)

**Topics Covered:**
- What Digital Literacy means and why it matters for students
- Safe Internet Practices (strong passwords, avoiding phishing links, not sharing personal data)
- Professional Online Presence (maintaining a clean digital footprint, using college email for sign-ups)

**Design Notes:**
- Single-page visual layout with clear headings and icons
- Used a clean blue-and-white colour scheme for a professional look
- Divided into three sections with brief bullet points under each

**Reflection (150–200 words):**

For this task, I used Canva to design a one-page digital literacy infographic targeted at first-year students. Canva's drag-and-drop interface made it easy to arrange elements without any design background — I selected a pre-built infographic template and customised the colours, text, and icons to match the three topics I chose: what digital literacy is, safe internet practices, and maintaining a professional online presence.

The most interesting part was thinking from the perspective of a peer who has never thought about digital safety before — it pushed me to keep the language simple and the visuals clear. I avoided technical jargon and focused on practical tips students can act on immediately, like using strong passwords and being careful about what they post publicly.

The most challenging part was deciding what to leave out. There is so much to cover under digital literacy that fitting everything onto one page required tough choices. In the end, I prioritised the three topics I felt were most immediately relevant to college students starting their academic journey online.
