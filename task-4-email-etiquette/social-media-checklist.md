# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic identity — especially on LinkedIn and GitHub.

2. **Use your college email for academic and professional sign-ups.** It adds credibility and keeps your personal and academic digital identities separate.

3. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

4. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all social media accounts.** This adds a critical layer of security against unauthorised access.

5. **Engage positively and constructively.** Share knowledge, congratulate peers, and participate in discussions that add value to your network.

6. **Credit others' work.** If you share or repost content — whether it's an article, photo, or idea — always acknowledge the original creator.

7. **Review your privacy settings regularly.** Limit who can see your personal information, location, and contact details on every platform.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly.** Avoid posting your home address, phone number, daily schedule, or financial details on any public platform.

2. **Don't post or share unverified news and rumours.** Always check the source before sharing — misinformation spreads fast and can have serious consequences.

3. **Don't engage in cyberbullying or online harassment.** Even seemingly harmless comments can cause real harm. Treat others online the way you would in person.

4. **Don't use social media during exams or important academic deadlines** in a way that distracts you — and don't post about exam questions or answers during active exams.

5. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers without verification.** Fake profiles are used for phishing, scams, and social engineering.

6. **Don't post about your location in real time.** Sharing live location updates (e.g., "at the library alone till midnight") can compromise your personal safety.

7. **Don't use the same password across platforms.** If one account is compromised, all others become vulnerable.

---

> Save this checklist and refer to it before making any major social media decisions.
